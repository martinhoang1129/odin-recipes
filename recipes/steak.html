Pan Seared Steak with Garlic Butter

Description

This cast iron pan seared steak recipe is a classic American dish, one that should be a staple in every household. The recipe sears a thick juicy cut of steak on a cast iron. The meat is seared using garlic butter with rosemary, salt, and pepper. This is a quick 15 minute recipe. It's so good that you want it eat it multiple times a week, but we recommend eating this recipe once every 2 weeks for health purposes.

Ingredients

  • new york strip or ribeye steak meat
  • salt & pepper
  • rosemary
  • garlic
  • butter
    •

    Steps

  • Bring meat to room temperature by taking it out the fridge for 30 minutes
  • season with salt & pepper on both sides. Don't be afraid to liberally season the steak as much of the salt & pepper is loss during searing
  • Heat cast iron skiller over medium high heat. Add cooking oil
  • Once Cast iron is hot and the oil is steaming, add the steak. Sear both sides for 3 minutes
  • After flipping the steak and searing for 3 minutes, add butter, garlic, rosemary. Sear each side for 1 minute
  • Measure the internal temperature using a meat thermometer for medium rare. When ready, let the steak cool down on a plate for 5 minutes and serve with your favorite sides!
